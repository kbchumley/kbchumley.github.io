About

Bixal Theory is a technology podcast built by Bixal for the ever growing tech, design, and open source community.

We do this by listening and learning from our employees, clients, and friends. Our goal is to help give back to the open source community with our own twist on the best methods and theories for powerful transformative technology.

Bixal is more than an organization. Bixal is a methodology. It’s a people-absolutely-first approach to solving complex organizational challenges, balancing cutting-edge technical chops with a deep sense of empathy and understanding.

Our Host

DJ Neace

Bixal Theory has many hands involved and many to thank for it’s creation but without our host DJ , we would just be a bunch of voices talking over eachother, rather the lovingly crafted podcast we have today!

DJ is from Big Stone Gap, Va. He received his B.S. in psychological research and M.A. in psychology from Southern Connecticut State University. Before coming to Bixal, he worked as a training coordinator at a nonprofit organization, spent two years as an investigator for a government contractor and conducted research at Yale University. DJ has earned certifications in e-Learning Instructional Designer, Instructional Design Program, Agile Project Management, 508 Compliance and Human-Centered Service Design. DJ is also the creator of the Lonesome Pine Podcast, which received an Audio Verse Award.

Color Theory
to Bixal Theory

Bixal Theory is a play off the design term “Color Theory”. Within color theory, understanding how to use different colors to convey meaning with appropriate accessibility is an important part of both design and marketing. At Bixal, similarly we believe that activating your strategic side and gaining knowledge of human centered design is a vital step towards creating impactful transformative technology. Bixal has a variety of brand colors that not only stands as a cohesive visual design unit but represents the diverse group of strategists, designers, engineers, and thinkers that we are.
